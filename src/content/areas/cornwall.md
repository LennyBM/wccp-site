## Cornwall — across the Tamar

Cornwall has been a core market for WCCP since the late 1970s. The Holsworthy yard sits ten miles from the border, which means we can put a HIAB on a Cornish dairy by mid-morning — most South West yards can't match that.

Our Cornish customer base is roughly 70% dairy and beef, 20% civils and arable, and 10% bespoke estate and tourism work. We've cast slurry stores across the Camel valley, retaining walls for the A30 corridor improvements, and bespoke shaped panels for several heritage estates near St Mawes.

## What we do across Cornwall

The pattern of Cornish farming — small-to-medium dairies with high stocking density and challenging slurry-to-pasture ratios — means SSAFO-compliant slurry storage is the single biggest product line we ship into the county. We've supplied dozens of SIG Round 3 jobs and expect Round 4 to be similarly busy.

We also see strong demand for cattle cubicles and feed troughs in the West Cornwall dairies (St Erth, Helston, Penzance) where pedigree Holstein herds are expanding under tighter welfare standards.

## Delivery and install

We run our own fleet as far as Hayle on a normal day, with single-load runs to Penzance and the Lizard. For Penwith deliveries we'll typically use a vetted local haulier from a transfer point near Truro to keep our own crews productive.

Install crews can be in mid-Cornwall within two hours and West Cornwall in around three. For larger jobs we'll often base a crew in a B&B for the week rather than commute daily.
